Greek Yogurt in the Thermomix TM-7

One-bowl method — heat, cool, ferment in the machine; strain for Greek texture. Batch: 2 L.

1 Equipment & ingredients

You need: Thermomix TM-7 (2.2 L bowl, Ferment mode) · kitchen thermometer · sieve + cheesecloth (or the Varoma dish lined with cloth) · clean jar.

Ingredients (2 L batch):

  • 2 L whole milk — preferably not UHT (UHT lets you skip the heating step).
  • 2–3 tbsp whole milk powder — optional, adds body.
  • Starter: 1/8 tsp (~0.5 g) Custom Probiotics #2 powder, or 3–4 tbsp (45–60 ml) yogurt from the previous batch.
2 L is the practical ceiling. The bowl is 2.2 L, so 2 L fits with little margin. When heating, remove the measuring cup and set the simmering basket on top to vent without splatter, and watch that the milk doesn't foam over. Straining loses ~half to whey, so 2 L yields a generous tub.

2 Step by step

1 · Heat the milk 85 °C · 10 min · Speed 2–3

Clean, dry bowl. Add milk (and milk powder if using). At 2 L you're near the bowl's ceiling — take the measuring cup off and set the basket on top so it vents without splatter, and keep an eye on it. The low speed keeps the milk moving so it won't scorch. Skip this step if using UHT milk.

2 · Cool to 40–43 °C

Let it cool ~30–40 min (a cold-water bath around the bowl base speeds it up). Confirm with the thermometer.

3 · Add the starter 4 sec · Speed 4

Powder: dissolve 1/8 tsp (~0.5 g) in a little warm milk first, then add. Or add 3–4 tbsp of previous yogurt. Mix briefly and gently — just enough to combine.

4 · Ferment Ferment mode · 40–43 °C · 12 h

Set the Ferment mode to 40–43 °C and the timer to 12 h (the per-cycle max). Lid on. Do not open or move it during fermentation.

5 · Chill, then strain for Greek texture

Transfer to a jar and refrigerate a few hours until cold (strains better cold). Then pour into a cloth-lined sieve over a bowl and drain in the fridge:
  • ~4 h → soft Greek
  • 12 h → thick Greek
  • 24 h → very thick / labneh
Critical: never add the starter above ~45 °C — the heat kills the live cultures. Always cool to 40–43 °C first.
Keep the whey. The strained liquid is rich in protein and probiotics — use it in smoothies or to soak oats.
